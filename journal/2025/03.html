Gaming

I finished the Half Life serie (except Half Life Alyx) and it was pretty good. I played the whole thing with my Valve obsessed very cool friend Miamoalex and I think having a true half life nerd in a call while playing the game really enhanced the experience, he had little anecdotes and trivia about the lore etc. It was really fun and it made some of the more tedious parts more enjoyable. I was very surprised by how good the first game was (i played Black Mesa, the fan-made remake, so that's probably why lol, but still). What shocked me was the fact i was able to play 2 to 3 hours of the same game without getting tired of it, which is very rare for me (i'm usually more of a "do something for 1 hour and switch activity" kind of person). When a game hook me like that i know it's good, and it kept getting better and better until the end of the serie and now before i knew it i was invested in the lore and making theories about the G-man and stuff. Now i get why people love thoses games so much and why they can stop talking about "half life 3" lol. Can't wait to play Alyx.

Keeping up with the gaming, we tried Repo and Peak with two of my dearest friends and god proximity chat games are fun. I cannot recommend them enough, if you have friends, try them out. Repo is like a goofy ah Lethal Company, equally scary and lethal and hard but with some really absurd stuff which makes it even better imo. And Peak is already a pretty fun and engaging game solo (it's about climbing a mountain) so it's even better with friends (still have to beat it tho, we died like 50m from the end....).

Also i started Deltarune back from the beginning like yesterday, litteraly cannot wait to get to chapters 3+4, hardly managed to not getting spoiled while going on socials ;-; But that's July's concerns!

Cinema

There was a japanese animated movie restrospective at the Paris Cinematheque last month (and still ongoing) and i went to see a few movies with friends. It was cool and deeefinitely refreshing since i haven't been to the cinema in a while lool.

  • Jin Roh the Wolf Brigade

    This one was cool but i was tired honestly and i fell asleep during an important plot point so i spent half of the movie trying to figure out the plot lol. Otherwise it was good but like mid tier good like i appreciated it but i i won't rewatch it or anything. Also I was obsessed with the fact that the main character looked like Gutz from Berserk.

    6.5/10

  • Angel's Egg

    So this one was WEIRD. But definitely a vibe like i really liked the art and the music and the global atmosphere and aesthetic and the environment etc. but the movie tells things in a very abstract way with almost only metaphors and biblical references. Also i think i was too tired (again) to handle the 5 minutes still shots of static images so i kinda closed my eyes a bit sometimes. Also the end scene is actually inspired by the end scene of Solaris by Tarkovsky!! that's cool

    6/10

  • Steamboy

    Surprisingly it's a movie by Katsuhiro Otomo, the guy who made Akira. I say surprisingly because the movie is VERY different from Akira. Firstly we don't have the dark tone of Akira, and we don't have it's mature and well structured plot either. Steamboy is clearly a children's movie but i wasn't really excepting that so i guess i was a bit disappointed lol. The image is good and the animation is amazing (one of the most expensive japanese animated movie ever made) but the story is very naive and has many plot holes and jumps from one point to another very quickly so [spoiler alert] it starts with some kind of mistery or political intrigue about a mysterious invention and then it builds up on that and builds up and builds up again and in the end we spend like 1 hour watching a big steampunk fight with a giant robot tower and we still don't clearly know what the invention is supposed to be lol.

    5/10

  • Millenium Actress

    Satoshi Kon being Satoshi Kon. Watch it.

    8/10
